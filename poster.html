🗳️ Problem & Motivation
  • Traditional voting systems are vulnerable to fraud, manipulation, and lack meaningful transparency for ordinary citizens.
  • Centralised e‑voting machines present a single point of failure — hackable and independently unverifiable.
  • Voters have no mechanism to confirm their vote was correctly recorded after casting.
  • Blockchain's immutability, decentralisation, and public auditability make it an ideal substrate for electoral systems.
 81% of citizens worry about election integrity Pew Research Center, 2020
🎯 Research Question
"To what extent can an Ethereum‑based smart contract system provide secure, transparent, and usable voting while maintaining voter privacy and scalability?"

💡 Conclusions & Outlook
  • Ethereum smart contracts successfully enforce transparent, tamper‑proof voting logic on‑chain.
  • Voter authorisation and double‑voting prevention function correctly across all test scenarios.
  • Usability feedback was highly positive (4.5/5); confirmation latency (~14 s) is the main UX friction.
Limitations
Plaintext votes (no privacy) · Gas costs on mainnet (~$0.22/vote) · No ranked‑choice voting
